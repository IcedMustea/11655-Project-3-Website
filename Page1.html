Green Tea

Green tea is the light, refreshing one of the group—barely oxidized, so it keeps that clean, grassy taste. It’s packed with antioxidants and gives a gentle, calm energy boost. Fun fact: It’s one of the oldest teas in the world, enjoyed for over 4,000 years!

Black Tea

Black tea is the bold, wake-you-up tea. It’s fully oxidized, which gives it its deep color and strong flavor. Great for energy and heart health, it’s the one people reach for in the morning. Fun fact: It’s the most commonly drunk tea worldwide—yes, even more than green tea!

Oolong Tea

Oolong sits right between green and black tea—smooth, floral, sometimes even creamy depending on how it’s made. Medium caffeine, super balanced, and great for metabolism. Fun fact: Some oolongs are rolled into tiny balls that slowly unfurl as they brew, like little tea flowers opening.
